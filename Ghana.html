Welcome to Ghana

Your gateway to exploring the vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, and stunning attractions of Ghana.

Meals in Ghana

  • Jollof Rice: A popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices, often served with chicken, beef, or fish.

  • Fufu: A starchy side dish made from cassava, yam, or plantain, pounded into a dough-like consistency and typically eaten with soups or stews like light soup, palm nut soup, or groundnut (peanut) soup.
  • Kelewele: Spicy fried plantains, seasoned with a mixture of ginger, cayenne pepper, and other spices, usually served as a snack or side dish.
  • Banku and Tilapia: Banku is a fermented corn and cassava dough, served with grilled tilapia fish, often accompanied by spicy pepper sauce or shito (a Ghanaian hot black pepper sauce).
  • Waakye: A nutritious dish made from rice and beans cooked together, often served with a variety of sides such as fried plantain, boiled eggs,fish,or meat.
  • Beans and Gari: A simple yet delicious dish made from cooked beans, often black-eyed beans, mixed with gari (grated and dried cassava) and palm oil served with fried ripe plantain.

Attractions in Ghana

  • Cape Coast Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this historical fort was a major hub of the trans-Atlantic slave trade. Visitors can tour the dungeons and learn about Ghana's colonial history.
  • Kakum National Park: Known for its lush rainforest and the famous canopy walkway that provides an aerial view of the forest and its wildlife, including monkeys and exotic birds.
  • Mole National Park: The largest national park in Ghana, home to elephants, antelopes, warthogs, and baboons. Visitors can go on safaris and nature walks.
  • Wli Waterfalls: The tallest waterfall in West Africa, located in the Volta Region, is a breathtaking natural attraction where visitors can hike and swim.
  • Lake Volta: One of the largest man-made lakes in the world, offering opportunities for boat cruises, fishing, and visits to the lakeside villages.


  • Elmina Castle: Another historical fort located in the Central Region of Ghana, important for its role in the trans-Atlantic slave trade.

Fun Facts About Ghana

  • Easter: Easter is marked by various cultural and religious events, including the famous Kwahu Easter celebrations in the Eastern Region, featuring paragliding, carnivals, and music festivals.
  • Kente Cloth: Ghana is famous for its vibrant and colorful Kente cloth, a traditional handwoven textile that originates from the Ashanti and Ewe people.
  • Christmas: Christmas in Ghana is celebrated with church services, feasts, and vibrant street parades. It's a time for family gatherings and community festivities.

  • Cultural Diversity: Ghana is home to over 100 ethnic groups, each with its unique traditions, languages, and festivals. Notable festivals include the Homowo festival of the Ga people and the Aboakyir festival of the Effutu people.
  • Music and Dance: Ghana is known for its vibrant music and dance traditions, including highlife, hiplife, and traditional drumming and dancing.
